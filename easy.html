What constitutes an easy hike?

This is arbitrary, but my ideal easy hike is just a walk around the park. So thats the benchmark here. I’d say anywhere less than 2 hours (three maximum). Little to no elevation gain. I think a good litmus test for what I think of when I think about this category is this: when your walking around could you at any moment just pack up and pretty easily get back to the car?

My top three easy hikes

King Phillip Overlook

Medfield, MA

King Philip Overlook (named after the war of the same name) is a convenient 2.6 mile loop with mild elevation gain. This rocky trail begins on a railroad track and summits at an overlook to the Charles river. Views of a model plane airfield can be seen below. If you come during the right times of day in the summer, you can see hobbyists flying planes and helicopters in the New England sunset. This is a safe, easy trail that still allows for some elevation gain. And at 1-1.5 hrs round trip, its a relaxing way to spend an afternoon.

Medfield State Hospital

Medfield, MA

Right on the other side of the Charles is the Medfield State Hospital. The abandoned mental asylum has been reinvigorated by the town as a dog walking park. You can spend plenty of time wandering around the prewar buildings and exploring the park. Be careful not to enter the buildings, though. Asbestos abounds. Fun fact: this is where the movie “Shutter Island” was filmed.

Wompatuck State Park

Hingham, MA

Theres a hidden diamond in Hingham, Massachusetts. Wompatuck State Park is an abandoned naval base (sensing a theme?). That features miles of walking trails. Though in the center of a relatively well-populated suburban area, Wompatuck feature secluded loops and bike-paths. There are trails that provide views of ponds, and others that take you around left-behind military bunkers—an eerie sight. Note of caution, the trail layout is exceptionally spiderwebbed and confusing, so it is very easy to get lost.
